NDIS Quality Assurance Platform

Clinical-grade evaluation for Behaviour Support Plans.

Caritas AI is a quality assurance and evaluation platform for NDIS Behaviour Support Plans. Built by practitioners, validated against expert clinical review, and designed to meet the regulatory standards set by the NDIS Quality and Safeguards Commission.

Request early access How it works
What it does

A complete workflow for writing, reviewing, and validating Behaviour Support Plans.

01 / BUILD

Draft evidence-based plans

Structured authoring across all twelve BSP sections, with evidence-based fallback content, de-identification of case data, and DOCX export. Designed to support practitioners, not replace them.

02 / EVALUATE

Quality-check against standards

Profile-based classification and domain-level scoring grounded in PBS best practice and NDIS regulatory requirements. Every finding traced back to the specific BSP section and evidence that triggered it.

03 / VALIDATE

Benchmark against expert review

Inter-rater reliability analysis comparing platform output to expert clinical scoring, using ICC, Cohen's kappa, and per-domain concordance — the same statistical standards as peer-reviewed clinical validation studies.

Who it's for

Built for everyone who reads, writes, or relies on a Behaviour Support Plan.

  • Behaviour support practitioners
    Authoring, peer review, and professional development support.
  • NDIS registered providers
    Quality assurance across a caseload, audit readiness, and evidence of continuous improvement.
  • Clinical supervisors
    Calibrated review of plans under supervision, with standardised scoring and feedback.
  • Researchers & regulators
    Structured evaluation data suitable for sector-wide quality benchmarking.
Clinical validation

Not another black box — measured against experts.

ICCInter-rater reliability framework

Every evaluation model running inside Caritas AI is calibrated against a panel of expert reviewers using the same statistical methods as peer-reviewed clinical validation studies — intraclass correlation, weighted kappa, and per-domain concordance.

We believe quality assurance tools that grade clinical practice should meet the same evidence standards as the practice they evaluate. A peer-reviewed validation study is in development.
